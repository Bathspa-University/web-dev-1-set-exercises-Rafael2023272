'Chopsuey Recipe'

Overview

ChopSuey is a stir-fried vegetable dish that is cooked with meats such as chicken and beef. Shrimp and seafood can also be added. This chop suey recipe is special because it has almost every ingredient present. Chicken, shrimp, and boiled quail eggs were all included in the recipe, along with fresh vegetables.

Ingredients:

  • 1 piece of boneless chicken breast, thinly sliced
  • 10 - 20 pieces of shrimp
  • 5 cups of assorted vegetables (carrots, bell peppers, snow peas, brocolli, cauliflower), sliced
  • 8 - 15 pieces of quail eggs
  • 1 1/2 cup cabbage, chopped
  • 1 onion, miced sliced
  • 1 garlic, mincedbsliced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 3 - 5 tablespoon oyster sauce, (Depends on your preference)
  • 1 tablespoon cornstarch, dissolved it in 2 tablespoons of water
  • A Pinch of Salt and pepper
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil
  • 1/2 cup of water

Instructions:

  1. Heat the cooking oil in a pan/wok over a medium heat.
  2. Add the chicken slices and cook it until it's lightly browned.
  3. Put the shrimp alongside with the chicken
  4. Cook the shrimp until it's turn color orange
  5. Set aside the chicken and shrimp
  6. Now Saut√© the garlic and onion until it fragrant.
  7. Add now the assorted vegetables and stir-fry it for 10 - 15 minutes. (Don't overcook the vegetables)
  8. Put the water, let it simmer for 2 minutes
  9. Put the chopped cabbage and cover the pan for 2 minutes
  10. Pour the liquid ingredients (soy sauce, oyster sauce, dissolved cornstarch) into the pan/wok and stir well until the sauce thickens.
  11. Add the salt and pepper to taste
  12. Put the boiled quail eggs as toppings
  13. turn off the heat and serve this with hot rice.
